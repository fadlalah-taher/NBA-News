It's officially the home stretch for professional basketball teams across the world, while March Madness is starting to heat up in the United States. As seasons come to an end, March will be a crucial month for prospects slated for the 2024 NBA draft. A pair of French players once again claim the top two spots, as small forward Zaccharie Risacher of JL Bourg in France maintains his place at No. 1 while Perth center Alex Sarr lands at No. 2. The duo to watch, though, could be a couple of Kentucky Wildcats who have a good case as top-five picks.
